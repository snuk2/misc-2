Dive Deeper Questions

After completing the lab exercises above, complete the following Dive Deeper questions. Remember, these questions are worth 3 points each and are graded for correctness.

1. Suppose you were to collect a new random sample of 2020 Netflix movies, but you were able to collect data for 240 movies (twice the sample size used above). If you found the same sample proportion (as you had in Exercise 5), would you expect the corresponding p-value to increase, decrease, or stay the same (as compared to the p-value from Exercise 7)? Explain your answer.

Answer: If we had collected the data for 240 movies instead of 120, the standard deviation of this distribution that we would calculate would become smaller (standard deviation of the normal distribution for a p-hat value is sqrt((p-hat - p-null) / sample size (n))). Since the standard deviation is smaller, we calculated the z-score or standardized score value to be larger because standard value = (p-hat - p-null) / standard deviation thus if standard deviation decreases the standard score increases. If the standardized score increases, the p-value or the probability of the sample proportion being greater than the p-hat value will decrease because larger standardized scores show more standard deviations away from the mean or center of the distribution, correlating to less probability of obtaining that sample proportion. Therefore, if the sample size increases, the corresponding p-value will decrease if p-hat remains the same.

2. Suppose you wish to run a similar test to evaluate if the population proportion of movies that are rated R has increased from a value of 0.17 (17%). After collecting a sample of movies, you find the sample proportion to be 0.07 (7%). Would you expect to find a small p-value or a large p-value based on these sample results? Explain your answer.

Answer: For these results, we would expect to find that the p-value is large if the sample proportion is 7% as compared to the assumed population proportion of 17%. This is because we are checking to see if the proportion of rated R movies has increased, but the sample proportion we find is 10% less than the null hypothesized population proportion of 17%. This means that the standardized score of the sample proportion will be less than the mean score because we are decreasing from the center of the proposed distribution. This means the z-score will be negative, and we are looking for the number of sample proportions that would fall above the current z-score we have, so we would get all sample proportions from the center to the right end of the distribution, indicating that our p-value or the probability of getting a sample proportion higher than 7% would be greater than 50%, causing the p-value to be large.
